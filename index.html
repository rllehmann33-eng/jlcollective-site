Why we exist

Your industry has been choosing between two bad options for years.

Option one: a generalist agency that cannot tell paint protection film from a vinyl wrap, learning your business on your budget, sending a monthly PDF nobody reads.

Option two: doing it yourself — at nine at night, between jobs, inside an ad platform expertly designed to spend your money faster than you can watch it.

We built the third option: an operations platform that never stops watching, run by people who take your trade seriously, under structures where we only get paid well when the work actually works.

02

The system

Every account runs on the same five-station loop. Every day.

We run paid media on an operations platform we built in-house — not rented agency software with our logo on it. This is the loop:

1

Watch

The platform monitors around the clock — spend pacing, ad health, performance against plan. Circuit breakers and budget guards are built into the machinery, standing behind every campaign the platform runs.

2

Prepare

It drafts the work: campaign plans, budget changes, creative rotations, weekly reports. Each action is prepared, priced, and queued — with its reasoning attached.

3

Approve

Nothing executes on its own. A person reviews every queued action and approves or declines it — from a desk or a phone. The approval is the ignition key, and it is never automated.

4

Act

Approved actions execute exactly as reviewed — and the guards check again at the moment of execution, not just at the moment of approval.

5

Account

Every action lands in a permanent audit log: who approved what, when, and what happened next. Reporting is generated from the record — not reconstructed from memory at the end of the month.

This is the difference between an agency that uses AI and an operating company that runs on it — with a human hand on the ignition.

06

Principles

Five things we do not bend on.

Aligned by structure, not by promise

Anyone can say incentives are aligned. We build it into the pricing, so the claim does not depend on our character on a bad day.

A human owns every decision

Automation earns its keep by preparing work and catching problems — never by acting unsupervised. Budgets, launches, and anything customer-facing get a human approval, every time.

Few partners, deep work

We take on a small number of businesses at a time and do not take on competing businesses inside the same market.

We operate what we build

Software handed over at launch starts dying at launch. We stay — hosting, support, improvements — because that is when the work starts paying.

The record is the truth

Every action is logged and every report is generated from that log. When we tell you what happened, it is not a recollection — it is the record.

Fair questions

Asked directly, answered directly.

Are you an AI company or a marketing agency?

An operating company. We built the software and we run the service — the machine does the relentless part; people make the judgment calls. If you want only the software or only the people, we are probably not your fit.

Will AI spend our money without asking?

No — and not because of a policy document. The platform is built so that any action touching an account must be approved by a person before it executes, and the guards check again at execution time. There is no switch that turns this off.

Who actually works on our account?

A named person you can call, backed by the platform. Not a rotating cast, not an offshore queue, not "your success team." One owner, on the record, accountable for the loop running on your account.

Do you work with our competitors?

Not inside the same market. It is part of keeping our partner list short: we would rather be unavailable than conflicted.

What does it cost?

It depends on scope, and we will not pretend otherwise. The audit exists so both of us can see the size of the opportunity before anyone talks numbers. Our structures typically pair a base fee with upside tied to results — which tells you what we expect of ourselves.

Can you build our app or platform too?

Yes — it is half of what we do. Quoting systems, lead pipelines, booking flows, full marketplace platforms. We design it, build it, and stay to operate it after launch.
