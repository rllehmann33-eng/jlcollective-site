Why we exist

Specialty industries have been underserved by both of their options.

The generalist agency treats a specialized business like any other account — learning the industry on your budget, reporting in templates, accountable to nothing in particular.

The do-it-yourself route trades your evenings to advertising platforms engineered to spend budget faster than they explain themselves.

We built the third option: an intelligent operations platform that never stops watching, directed by people who know the industry, under structures that tie our reward to what the work produces.

02

The system

Every account runs on one operating loop. Continuously.

We operate paid media on a platform built entirely in-house — proprietary infrastructure, not rented agency software. Five stages govern everything:

1

Watch

The platform monitors continuously — spend pacing, creative health, performance against plan — with circuit breakers and budget governors engineered into the foundation.

2

Prepare

It composes the work: campaign plans, budget adjustments, creative rotations, reporting. Every action is prepared, priced, and queued — with its reasoning attached.

3

Approve

Nothing executes autonomously. A person reviews every queued action and approves or declines it. That approval is the gate, and it is never automated.

4

Act

Approved actions execute precisely as reviewed — verified again at the moment of execution, not only at the moment of approval.

5

Account

Every action enters a permanent audit record: who approved it, when, and what followed. Reporting is generated from the record — never reconstructed from memory.

06

Principles

Five commitments we do not bend.

Aligned by structure, not by promise

Anyone can claim aligned incentives. We encode them in the pricing, so the claim survives contact with a difficult quarter.

Human judgment holds final authority

Automation prepares the work and catches the problems. It does not act unsupervised. Budgets, launches, and anything customer-facing receive human approval — every time, on the record.

Few partners, deep work

A deliberately small roster, and no competing engagements inside the same market.

We operate what we build

Software abandoned at delivery begins failing at delivery. We stay — hosting, support, improvement — because operation is where the work compounds.

The record is the truth

Every action is logged, and every report is generated from that log. What we tell you happened is not a recollection — it is the record.

Fair questions

Asked directly. Answered directly.

Are you an AI company or a marketing agency?

An operating company. We build the technology and we run the service: the platform does the relentless part; people make the judgment calls. You engage one firm and receive both.

Will AI spend our budget without approval?

No — and not as a matter of policy. The platform is architected so that any action affecting an account must receive human approval before it executes, with verification repeated at the moment of execution. No configuration disables this.

Who actually works on our account?

A named principal, backed by the platform — not a rotating team, not a ticketing queue. One owner, accountable for the loop running on your account, reachable directly.

Do you work with our competitors?

Not within the same market. We would rather be unavailable than conflicted.

What does it cost?

It depends on scope, and we decline to pretend otherwise. The audit exists so both parties can see the size of the opportunity before discussing terms. Our structures typically pair a base fee with performance-linked upside — which tells you what we expect of ourselves.

Can you build our platform as well?

Yes — it is half of our practice. Quoting systems, lead pipelines, booking flows, and full marketplace platforms: designed, built, and operated.
